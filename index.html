About Me

I am a third-year PhD student at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, in Barcelona - Spain, where I am pursuing a PhD in Computer Science.

Currently, I am also a Data Scientist at Skyscanner, where I develop recommender systems, learning-to-rank algorithms and Bayesian approaches to hypothesis testing.

Research

I focus my research on both, theoretical and applied machine learning.

On the theoretical side, I work on the theory of spectral methods for learning latent variable models. I am particularly interested in providing theoretical advancements aiming at improving the current understanding of these methods as well as their usability on real world data.

On the application side, I am collaborating with Catalan Institute of Health, applying spectral methods of moments to large-scale healthcare data.
