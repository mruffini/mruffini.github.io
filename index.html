About Me

I am a third-year PhD student at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya, in Barcelona - Spain, where I am pursuing a PhD in Computer Science.

Currently, I am also Data Scientist at Skyscanner, developing recommender systems, learning-to-rank algorithms and Bayesian approaches to hypothesis testing.

Research

I focus my research on both, theorehical and applied machine learning.

My research focuses on the theory of spectral methods for learning latent variable models. I am particularly interested in providing theoretical advancements aiming at improving the current understanding of these methods, providing data-based perspectives on their functioning and improving their usability on real world data.

I am also actively collaborating with Catalan Institute of Health, focusing on applying spectral methods of moments to large-scale healthcare data.
